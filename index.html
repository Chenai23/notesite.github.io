WHAT IS ORGANIC CHEMISTRY?


Overview

Organic chemistry is a branch of chemistry that is concerned with carbon and its compounds.
It studies the structure, properties, composition, reactions, and preparation of carbon-containing compounds,
especially those that have the carbon-hydrogen bond. Organic chemistry is also the basis of biochemistry, as all life on earth is carbon-based.
Organic compounds may include other elements besides carbon and hydrogen, such as nitrogen, oxygen, halogens, phosphorus, silicon, and sulfur.

Classification of Compounds

Depending upon the arrangement of carbon atoms in their structure, organic compounds are broadly categorized into:

- Acyclic or Open Chain compounds
- Cyclic or Closed Chain compounds

Types of Reactions

There are mainly five types of organic reactions:

(1)Substitution reaction
(2)Elimination reaction
(3)Addition reaction
(4)Radical reactions
(5)Oxidation-Reduction Reactions.
